RTP — Almost Identical

At 96.52% vs 96.50%, this is essentially a draw. Both are above the industry average of 96%. Gates of Olympus 1000 can be found at reduced RTP versions (94.50%, 95.51%) at certain casinos — always check before playing. Fury of Anubis currently publishes one standard RTP. Edge: Fury of Anubis (marginally, and more consistent across casinos).

Volatility — The Biggest Difference

This is where the games diverge most. Fury of Anubis is medium volatility (3/5) — wins come more regularly, sessions last longer on the same bankroll, and the swings are real but not brutal. Gates of Olympus 1000 is high volatility (4/5) — expect longer dry spells, bigger swings, and a game that demands a larger bankroll buffer.

If you have $100 to play with, Fury of Anubis gives you a more sustainable session. If you have $500 and want the biggest possible swings, Gates of Olympus 1000 is the hunt.

Multiplier Mechanics — Different Systems

Fury of Anubis uses a doubling multiplier — starts at ×1, doubles with every tumble up to ×1,024. In free spins it resets to your starting value (×8–×64) before each spin.

Gates of Olympus 1000 uses an additive multiplier system — multiplier symbols land on the grid and their values are added together, then applied to the total win. The maximum combined multiplier is ×1,000 (hence the name). There's no doubling chain — it's about landing enough multiplier symbols in a single tumble sequence.

Fury of Anubis's doubling system rewards tumble chains. Gates of Olympus 1000 rewards multiplier symbol density. Both can produce enormous wins from a single spin — the path there is just different.

Max Win — Gates Takes It

Gates of Olympus 1000's 25,000× max win is 2.5× higher than Fury of Anubis's 10,000×. For pure ceiling hunters, Gates wins here. But that higher ceiling comes with considerably longer odds and higher volatility — you're paying for that extra potential in session variance.

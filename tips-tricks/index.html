Fury of Anubis – Tips, Tricks & Strategy

Bankroll management, bonus buy decisions, gamble feature strategy, and the myths that cost players money — all covered.

Beginner Strategy

If you're new to scatter-pay multiplier slots, start here. The core principle is simple: give the game enough spins to reach the bonus. A single free spins trigger with a decent multiplier can return 50–200× in a good session. Getting there is the game.

1
Start at Min Bet
Begin at $0.20 per spin. Learn the game rhythm — how often wins come, how multipliers build — before scaling up. The mechanics are identical regardless of stake.
2
Set a Session Limit
Decide your maximum loss before you spin. Stick to it. The bonus hits 1 in 435 spins naturally — budget for that variance before entering a session.
3
Don't Chase
The RTP is 96.52% over millions of spins. Short-term sessions deviate significantly. If you hit your limit, stop — no spin is more likely to produce a bonus than the last.
4
Use Demo First
Play 100+ free demo spins before wagering real money. Understand how the tumble multiplier builds and how the gamble feature works before it costs you.

Gamble Feature — When to Accept, When to Double

Before free spins begin, you're offered a 50/50 gamble: double your starting multiplier or lose half. Here's how to think about each scenario:

Always Gamble
3-scatter trigger (×8): The worst-case outcome is ×4 — still a meaningful free spins round. The upside is ×16. Risk/reward strongly favours gambling here.
Usually Gamble
4-scatter trigger (×16): Doubling to ×32 is significant. Dropping to ×8 is acceptable. Most experienced players gamble this one.
Situational
5-scatter trigger (×32): Doubling to ×64 is massive. Dropping to ×16 still gives a solid session. Decision depends on your bankroll situation.
Think Carefully
6-scatter trigger (×64): Gambling to ×128 or ×256 is life-changing potential — but dropping to ×32 after landing 6 scatters naturally is a painful outcome. Only gamble if you can afford it emotionally and financially.

Bonus Buy — Which Option Is Best?

🎯 Best Value: Random (175×)
Costs 175× for a random multiplier between ×8–×64. Statistically the best balance of cost vs. expected starting multiplier across many buys.
⚡ High Ceiling: Free Spins 4 (800×)
Guarantees ×64 starting multiplier. Most expensive but also the highest floor. For max-win hunters, this is the intended route — especially combined with gambling to ×128 or ×256.
💸 Budget Option: Free Spins 1 (100×)
Cheapest entry point at 100× your bet. Starting multiplier of ×8 means the bonus is workable but requires more tumbles to reach big multiplier territory.
🔥 Max Win: Super Spin 2 (250×)
A single spin with guaranteed ×256 starting multiplier. The highest theoretical single-spin payout. Not a session strategy — this is one-shot max-win hunting.

Myths vs Reality

These misconceptions cost players money. Here's the truth.

✗
Myth: "The slot is hot/cold after a big win"
Reality: Each spin is independent. RNG means the outcome of spin #501 is in no way influenced by spin #500. There is no memory in the machine.
✗
Myth: "Changing bet size forces a bonus"
Reality: Bet size affects payout amounts but not trigger probability. A $1 spin and a $10 spin have identical chances of triggering free spins (except with Ante Bet).
✗
Myth: "The 96.52% RTP means I'll get back $96.52 per $100"
Reality: RTP is a theoretical long-run average across billions of spins. In a 100-spin session you might return 30% or 300% — variance is the entire point of the game.
✗
Myth: "Bonus Buy has better odds than base game"
Reality: Bonus Buy guarantees entry into the feature, but it doesn't change the RTP of the free spins themselves. It's a convenience — not a cheat code.

100 spins at default bet, no Ante Bet, no Bonus Buy. Track how many wins you get, how often multipliers chain above ×8, and how long between bonuses.
B
Session 2: Ante Bet Test
100 spins with Ante Bet active. Compare bonus frequency vs. Session 1. You'll likely see significantly more triggers — judge if the 3× cost feels worth it.
C
Session 3: Bonus Buy Comparison
Buy 10 bonuses across different tiers (100×, 175×, 400×). Compare average returns. This gives you a personal feel for the variance within each tier.
D
Session 4: Gamble Feature
Every time you trigger a bonus, take the gamble. Over 10+ triggers you'll build intuition for the risk distribution. Always double-gambling will feel very different to never gambling.
